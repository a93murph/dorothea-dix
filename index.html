Dorothea Dix

Fighting for Mental Patients

  • Dix was born in Hampden, Maine in 1802.
  • She set out to tour mental hospitals across the country. She often reported her findings to several politicians.
  • When the Civil War started, Dix completely dedicated herself to the Union cause. She was designated as the Superintendent of Army Nurses for the Union Army.
  • Dix was also known for treating both Confederate and Union soldiers.
  • During a period when male doctors openly expressed disdain for female nurses, Dix continued to push for formal training and more opportunities for women nurses.
  • After the war, Dix raised funds for the building of a national monument to honor deceased soldiers, which stands at Fort Monroe, Virginia today.
  • She died on July 17, 1887 and is buried in Cambridge Massachusetts.
